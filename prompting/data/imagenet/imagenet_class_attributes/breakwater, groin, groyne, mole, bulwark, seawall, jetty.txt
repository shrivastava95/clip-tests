- Location: coastal area or shoreline
- Structure: solid barrier or wall
- Material: typically made of concrete, rocks, or wood
- Purpose: to protect the shore from erosion or wave action
- Shape: can vary from straight to curved
- Length: can extend into the water or run parallel to the shoreline
- Height: can be low or high depending on the intended function
- Presence of gaps or openings: may have gaps or spaces between the structure
- Additional structures: may have additional features like a promenade or walkway
- Presence of steps or stairs: may have steps or stairs leading to the water
- Presence of lighthouses or navigation aids: may have lighthouses or markers for ships
- Surrounding environment: may be surrounded by sand, rocks, or vegetation
