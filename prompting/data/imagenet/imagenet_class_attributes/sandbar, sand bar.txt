- A long, narrow strip of sand
- Located in a body of water, such as a river or ocean
- Usually visible during low tide
- Separates the water from the shore
- May have vegetation or debris on top
- Can be curved or straight in shape
- May have a gradual slope or steep drop-off
- Can vary in size and length
- Often formed by sediment deposition
- May have wave patterns or ripples on the surface
